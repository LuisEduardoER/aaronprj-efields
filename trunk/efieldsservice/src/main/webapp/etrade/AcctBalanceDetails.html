Net Cash / Margin Balance
This figure represents the total real-time dollar amount of all credit, debit, and other cash equivalent balances in your account. If this value is negative, you are borrowing on margin. However, the amount subject to interest charges or credits may be different if funds are still in settlement from trading activity.

Note: Proceeds from the short sale of stock are not included in this balance and not eligible to pay down a margin debit balance.
Funds withheld from Purchasing Power
This figure represents the total dollar amount of recent deposits that are not yet available for investing. Check deposits and certain cash transfers require time to clear and are not immediately included in your Purchasing Power, but will accrue interest immediately.

Even though these funds are not yet available for investment, they are reflected in your Net Cash / Margin Balance as soon as your deposit is received. See the Deposit Details table below for information on individual deposits.
Funds withheld from Withdrawal
This figure represents the total dollar amount of recent deposits that are not yet available for withdrawal. Check deposits and certain cash transfers require time to clear and are not immediately included in your Max Available for Withdrawal, but will accrue interest immediately.

Even though these funds are not yet available for withdrawal, they are reflected in your Net Cash/Margin Balance as soon as your deposit is received. See the Deposit Details table below for information on individual deposits.
Margin Balance
A positive Margin Balance figure represents the total real-time dollar amount of cash in the account, which is not currently invested in a Sweep Deposit Account or other sweepable money market fund because:
  • No sweep fund has been selected
  • Funds are still in settlement from trading activity
  • Funds are reserved for margin requirements for options
A negative balance figure represents a real-time margin debit balance, not adjusted for funds in a Sweep Deposit Account or other sweepable money market fund. To determine the actual amount borrowed on margin, see Net Cash / Margin Balance.

Note: Proceeds from the short sale of stock are not included in this balance and are not eligible to pay down a margin debit balance.
Cash Balance
This figure represents the total real-time dollar amount of cash in the account, which is not currently invested in a Sweep Deposit Account or other sweepable money market fund because:
  • No sweep fund has been selected
  • Funds are still in settlement from trading activity
A negative balance here may reflect an unsettled trade debit that has not yet been covered by available Sweep Deposit Account or other sweepable money market funds. This could also be an amount owed when no other cash equivalents are available. To determine the aggregate amount of all cash and cash equivalents, see Net Cash Balance.
Marginable Securities
The maximum dollar value of marginable securities you can purchase based on the Federal Reserve Initial Margin Requirement of 50% and E*TRADE’s standard house Margin Maintenance Requirement of 30%. Any portion of this amount above and beyond your Net Cash Balance will be on Margin, and you will be charged interest for the portion you have borrowed from E*TRADE.

Purchasing power may fluctuate during the day, as the market value and house maintenance requirements of your holdings vary with market prices. E*TRADE has set higher house maintenance requirements for certain stocks and your purchasing power for those stocks may be less than the amount shown. Purchasing power is also reduced for open orders.
Non-marginable Securities/Options
The maximum dollar value of non-marginable securities or options you can purchase, using both your cash and the loan value of marginable positions in the account. Any portion of this amount above and beyond your Net Cash Balance will be on Margin, and you will be charged interest for the portion you have borrowed from E*TRADE.

Purchasing power may fluctuate during the day, as the market value and house maintenance requirements of your holdings vary with market prices. Purchasing power is also reduced for open orders.
Day Trading Purchasing Power
This represents the maximum dollar value of marginable securities you can purchase for day-trading. This purchasing power is generally greater than your “regular”, or “overnight” margin purchasing power, and is intended for the purchase of securities that will be liquidated on the same day as the purchase. Day Trading Purchasing Power is based on your excess equity calculated at start of day, but may be reduced during the market session if your positions lose value or if you lose money on your completed day-trades. This lost buying power may be restored if your holdings subsequently gain in value, but your Day Trading Purchasing Power will never exceed the start-of-day value.

Overnight, your account must still satisfy the Federal Reserve initial margin requirement of 50% and E*TRADE’s house margin maintenance requirements of 30% or higher. Day-trading accounts that fail to comply with these overnight requirements may be subject to a Federal or House Maintenance margin call. Day Trading and regular buying power values are reduced to reflect open orders.
